## What is PWA and why do we need it ?

They are a mixture of web apps and mobile applications. Example - some business are app specific. Eg - Uber. Uber also has a web version but hardly people use it.
Some applications like Google Docs, are websites, it makes sense to use these on the webs.

There are some pros of websites, and some pros for apps. Both have advantages and drawbacks.

PWAs were launched recently. Google pushed for this. They are a mix of both worlds. Web apps and mobile apps.

## Problems solved by PWAs 

1. **Cost of development** is higher for having andriod, ios app and a web version. This was solved by PWAs.

2. **Discoverability** - Apps are not as easy to market compared to websites. Websites are easy to access and reach out to customers.

3. **Installation** - Register on playstore, download app, register inside the app. There is a customer drop off in every step of the app. SEO is absent in the case of applications.

## Problems in websites

1. Seamless experience - After few days customers tend to forget the websites they visited. Customers are not loyal to websites as loyal they are to mobile applications. Apps have easy access as they are present in our home screen. Seamless experience.

2. Offline support/ push notifications

#### Pwa's tries to solve of this by making a mixture of web and mobile apps.

PWAs are like websites which looks, behaves and acts like an app. They are built using html css js. But they behave like a mobile applications.
### Advantages

1. Easy to discover - google search
2. SEO of PWA apps is high.
3. Have access to home screen - We can click a button and install the app from the website and it will act as an app on our mobile home screen.
4. Offline support and experience.
5. Push notifications
6. Background sync - If we sent messages when we are offline, the message is still there when we close the app. When we turn on internet, the message is still sent later in the background. When we are offline also background syncing is happening. This is a major advantage of PWA.
7. Look and feel - UX. PWAs are mostly built to use it on phones. 
8. Responsive and mobile first.
9. No upfront installation cost.
10. Access to device apps (Eg- Camera, file system) - seamless experience.
### Easy to get users on a PWA than on an app

##### Since PWAs have offline support, we very well know what is being used to achieve this. It is service workers. Service workers are used to make PWAs offline.

They are nothing but websites which behave like apps.

### Manifest.json

Important file for making PWAs.
It is kind of like a configuration file.
It gives an option on the website to download the PWA version of the application.
